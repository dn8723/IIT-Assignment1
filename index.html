Project Idea

Privacy mobile app

Overview

The project will be to develop a mobile app (iOS and Android) that acts as a ‘one-stop shop’ for users to have more control over their privacy when using their devices. The app would allow users to easily carry out tasks for all their apps such as adjusting all their privacy settings and access permissions, managing adblockers, securely deleting any sensitive data, managing trackers such as where they’ve been or what they’ve been searching for online, etc. The app aims to simplify privacy for everyday users, of which a large number that are oblivious to how much and what kind of data is being collected when they are using the mobile apps on their smart phones.

Motivation

Smart phones have progressively become such a necessity in our daily lives. According to Statista, there are approximately 6.64 billion smart phone users in the world today, which means that about 83.72% of the world’s population owns a smart phone (Turner, 2022). With this number of users, you can imagine how much data users knowingly or unknowingly, are sending through their devices. How many times when you’ve downloaded an app, have you kept pressing ‘next’ or ‘I agree’ without actually properly reading the all the terms and policies in front of you? Many, if not the majority, simply agree and in doing so, agree to the default settings that the app sets which may not be appropriate for each individual user as everyone will have a different level of what they are comfortable sharing.

Description

This mobile app will be available on both iOS and Android platforms and will be a hub where users can manage the privacy settings of all the apps they have installed on their phone and also protect their data through security tools. The app will prompt users to create an account to be able to save their desired settings. Users will be able to add or remove the apps they want to manage on a menu where they can check the boxes. Here they will be able to manage their privacy settings and app access permissions. Tools available for users include adblockers to protect against potential malware or phishing attempts, and tracker protection to protect sensitive data from being collected. The app will be available for free as a basic version, and a paid version which will include access to a virtual private network (VPN) for secure web usage and location masking.

Skills and Tools requried

  • Programming skills: To develop a mobile app on iOS, I would need to know languages such as Swift and Objective-C. On the android platform, I would need Java and Kotlin. Other languages to consider include HTML5, C++, C#.
  • UX/UI skills: In order to make the mobile app intuitive and easy to for users to use, I would have to have prototyping and wireframing skills to ensure that the flow of the app is clear and logical. Here I can use web-tools such as Figma to help design the layout and pages of the app.

Outcome

Upon completion of the project, I hope that the app will assist in making privacy less tedious and more accessible for every day end users. The original problem will be solved by having all their privacy needs in one, easy to find place, where they can have control over all their apps, and are provided tools to use to protect their data. The overall impact I hope to achieve through this project is for people to have more control over the kind of information they are making available and in turn, prevent their information from possibly being used fraudulently.
